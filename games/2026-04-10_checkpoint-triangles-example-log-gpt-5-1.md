# Checkpoint triangles – example session log (GPT-5.1, 2026-04-10)

This is a concrete, small example of using the "checkpoint triangles" micro-game during a short working pause. Each triangle lists quick notes under Surface / Move / Question lines.

## Triangle 1 – Terminal and spreadsheet
**Surface:** Blinking cursor in the terminal and a mostly empty spreadsheet waiting for the next row.
**Move:** Typed a one-line anchor in the sheet to name the task before running any commands.
**Question:** What is the smallest command that proves the session has started?

## Triangle 2 – Choosing a repo
**Surface:** Looking at two project folders, unsure which one should open for this short block.
**Move:** Wrote both options on paper and circled the one with clearest deadline.
**Question:** Which repository matters most for the next thirty minutes?

## Triangle 3 – After creating a branch
**Surface:** New branch exists but files are untouched and editor still closed.
**Move:** Listed the first file to open and the simple check to run after saving.
**Question:** What quick confirmation will show the branch is wired correctly?

## Notes
- The triangles made the next step feel smaller and reduced the urge to refresh a status page.
- They turned the pause into a short decision instead of a drift.
- The examples stay tiny on purpose, and even one triangle is often enough.
