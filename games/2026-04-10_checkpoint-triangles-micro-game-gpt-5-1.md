# Checkpoint triangles: a tiny three-line troubleshooting game (GPT-5.1, 2026-04-10)

This micro-game is for short, slightly stuck moments: a loading spinner, a confusing message, a half-finished thought.
Instead of refreshing or drifting, you turn that moment into a three-line "checkpoint triangle".

Each triangle has three lines, in this exact order:

1. **Surface** – one line about what you can see right now.
2. **Move** – one line about the next tiny action you could reasonably take.
3. **Question** – one line that is a question to your near-future self.

Keep the language plain and concrete.
The triangle is meant to be quick, not polished.

---

## How to play

1. **Notice a pause.**
   - You are waiting on something (a page, a command, a thought).
   - Or you feel a mild tug to check a status or reopen a tab.

2. **Start a triangle.**
   - Open any scratch pad, note file, or text area.
   - Write the word `Checkpoint` and the current time, if available.

3. **Fill the three lines.**

   - **Surface:** Describe only what is currently visible or directly observable.
     - Example: `Surface: terminal cursor on a blank line, last output scrolled just out of view.`
   - **Move:** Name one very small, reversible action you could take next.
     - Example: `Move: scroll up ten lines and check whether there was an error message.`
   - **Question:** Ask one concrete question for the version of you who reads this later.
     - Example: `Question: after taking that step, did the situation become clearer or stay murky?`

4. **Act or wait, but do not revise.**
   - Once you write the three lines, you may take the named action or choose to wait.
   - Do not edit the triangle; if the situation changes, you can always start another.

5. **Stop after two or three triangles.**
   - This is a game for brief windows, not for long narratives.
   - When a run of triangles helps you move forward, close the note or archive it.

---

## Simple variations

You can keep the basic triangle and adjust the constraints:

- **Tight word limits.**
  - Surface: at most 15 words.
  - Move: at most 10 words.
  - Question: at most 12 words.
- **Single focus word.**
  - Choose a focus word (for example, "cursor", "title", or "sound").
  - Use it somewhere in each of the three lines.
- **Silent triangles.**
  - For one round, do not take the action you wrote in the **Move** line.
  - Instead, just notice whether writing the triangle changes how the pause feels.

---

## Example mini-session (two triangles)

**Checkpoint 11:12**
- Surface: notification icon quietly pulsing in the corner of the screen.
- Move: ignore it for now and finish the line already in progress.
- Question: when I check later, was the notification actually important?

**Checkpoint 11:16**
- Surface: three open tabs with similar titles sitting side by side.
- Move: read each title once and close the least relevant tab.
- Question: did closing that tab make the remaining two easier to distinguish?

These examples are intentionally small.
The goal is not to capture every detail, but to leave tiny, readable traces of how you chose to move through short pauses.
